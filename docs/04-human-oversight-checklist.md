# Human Oversight Checklist

This checklist defines the minimum human oversight expectations for the AI-assisted resume screening use case. The purpose is to ensure that AI outputs are reviewed critically, that final decisions remain under human responsibility, and that unreliable or unjustified outputs are not used without review.

## Oversight Objective

The objective of human oversight is to ensure that the system is used as a decision-support tool rather than an automated decision-maker. Human reviewers are expected to validate AI-generated scores and rankings, identify potentially unfair or unreliable outcomes, and intervene when the output is weakly justified, insufficiently explained, or inconsistent with the candidate’s actual qualifications.

## Human Review Is Required When

- A candidate receives a very low score.
- A candidate receives an unusually high score.
- A rejection, exclusion, or negative screening outcome is being considered.
- The system’s explanation is weak, missing, or insufficient.
- Resume information is incomplete, ambiguous, or difficult to interpret.
- The score appears to rely heavily on repeated keywords.
- The output appears inconsistent with the qualifications shown in the resume.
- There are signs that non-job-related attributes may have influenced the result.

## Reviewer Checklist

Before relying on the system’s output, the reviewer should ask:

- Is the score supported by job-relevant skills, qualifications, and experience?
- Is there enough evidence in the resume to justify the ranking?
- Could the score have been affected by missing or ambiguously presented information?
- Could repeated job-related keywords have inflated the score?
- Does the explanation clearly identify the main factors that contributed to the result?
- Does the output appear fair and reasonable when compared with the actual resume content?
- Are there signs that non-job-related proxy attributes may have influenced the score?
- Should this case be escalated for further review?

## Reviewer Actions

When concerns are identified, the reviewer may take one or more of the following actions:

- Confirm the system output if it appears justified and sufficiently explained.
- Override the score or ranking if the output appears unreliable, unfair, or weakly supported.
- Mark the case for additional review.
- Escalate the case to a more senior reviewer or HR lead.
- Disregard the AI output if it cannot be meaningfully justified.
- Record the reason for any override, escalation, or rejection of the system output.

## Documentation Requirements

The following information should be documented when human review is performed:

- The reviewer’s role or identifier
- The date of the review
- Whether the AI output was accepted, overridden, or escalated
- The reason for the reviewer’s decision
- Any identified issue, such as weak explanation, possible unfairness, incomplete information, or keyword-driven inflation
- Any follow-up action taken

## Oversight Limitations

Human oversight reduces risk, but it does not eliminate it entirely. The following limitations may remain:

- Human reviewers may rely too heavily on AI-generated outputs.
- Review quality may vary across reviewers.
- Time pressure may reduce the depth of manual review.
- Some unfair or weakly justified outcomes may still appear convincing.
- Indirect bias or proxy effects may remain difficult to detect.
- Human oversight may become inconsistent if expectations are not clearly defined and documented.

## Final Oversight Principle

The AI system must not be treated as the final decision-maker in screening decisions. Human reviewers remain responsible for validating outcomes, identifying questionable results, and ensuring that screening decisions are justified, explainable, and aligned with the intended use of the system.
