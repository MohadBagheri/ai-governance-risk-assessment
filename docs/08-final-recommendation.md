# Final Recommendation – HR Resume-Screening AI Assistant

## Overall Assessment

The AI system provides potential value in supporting HR screening processes by improving efficiency and consistency. 
However, it operates in a high-impact domain (hiring), where risks such as bias, lack of explainability, and overreliance on AI outputs can significantly affect individuals.

As currently designed, the system presents moderate to high risk due to the absence of formal governance structures, measurement frameworks, and incident response mechanisms.

---

## Deployment Decision

The system may be deployed as a decision-support tool under strict conditions. 
Deployment should only proceed if key risks such as bias, lack of explainability, and overreliance on AI outputs are addressed through improved governance, measurement, and monitoring mechanisms. 
Human oversight must remain mandatory, and AI outputs should not be used as the sole basis for hiring or rejection decisions.

---

## Key Risks

The most critical risks associated with this system include:

- Bias in candidate scoring, which may lead to unfair or discriminatory outcomes  
- Lack of explainability, limiting transparency and understanding of AI-driven decisions  
- Overreliance on AI outputs by HR reviewers, potentially reducing critical human judgment  
- Privacy risks related to the handling and processing of candidate data  

---

## Recommended Actions

To improve the system’s alignment with AI governance best practices, the following actions are recommended:

- Define clear governance roles and responsibilities (e.g., AI system owner, HR reviewer, compliance)  
- Establish formal AI governance and usage policies  
- Introduce performance, fairness, and explainability metrics  
- Implement a structured risk prioritization framework based on impact and likelihood  
- Develop and document an incident response and monitoring process  
- Ensure data minimization and proper handling of candidate data to address privacy risks  

---

## Conditions for Safe Use

The system should only be used under the following conditions:

- AI outputs must not be used as the sole basis for hiring or rejection decisions  
- Human review must be mandatory for all final decisions  
- High-risk or low-confidence outputs should be flagged for manual review  
- Regular monitoring and evaluation of system performance and fairness must be conducted  
- Identified risks must be periodically reassessed and mitigated
